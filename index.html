jquery.greekkeys-convert.js

What is it?

jquery.greekkeys-convert.js is jQuery plugin for converting legacy GreekKeys-encoded text into Unicode. If you had a large amount of GreekKeys encoded text that you wanted to put on a web page, with this plugin you can paste it directly into your HTML and convert it automatically into Unicode.

GreekKeys is a popular customized keyboard with accompanying fonts for composing Classical Greek first developed for the Macinstosh in 1984 by George B. Walsh, now maintained by Donald Mastronarde and distributed by the American Philological Association. Due to its popularity, many pre-Unicode Greek fonts followed its mapping of characters. In 2005, GreekKeys switched to Unicode, but there are still over two decades worth of documents in the old encoding.

jquery.greekkeys-convert.js has evolved itself from an original Perl version of an online converter I created sometime around 1998.

How to get it

  • Download Version 1.0. Contains full (22K) and minified (6.4K) versions.
  • Fork the project from GitHub.

How to use it

The plugin creates a greekkeys2utf8 object with one method, convert(), which you can apply to any selector.

jquery.greekkeys-convert.js automatically combines some characters that are uncombined in GreekKeys. There is a combine option to turn this off.

jquery.greekkeys-convert.js will generate characters not on this chart when it combines some glyphs, mostly capital letters and diacritial marks. For instance, GreekKeys has no mapping for a capital alpha with rough breathing and acute accent. You would render that with two characters: † (0x86), followed by A (0x41). When those characters occur by themselves (as they do here) they are converted into the equivalent Unicode characters 0x1fde and 0x0391, respectively. When they occur together, they will be combined into the single Unicode character 0x1f0d: †A.
